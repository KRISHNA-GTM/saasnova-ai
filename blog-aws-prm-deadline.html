The July 31 AWS PRM Deadline: How to Measure Attribution, Keep Funding, and Avoid Co-Sell Delays

Jen Dawson
April 2026 5 min read

Are you an AWS APN partner? Have you heard of PRM yet? By July 31, 2026, the way AWS "sees" your value changes forever. If you aren't visible in the new Partner Revenue Measurement (PRM) system, your co-sell engine doesn't just slow down — it stops.

For most APN partners, PRM is the most important deadline you haven't been briefed on yet. AWS has introduced PRM as a mandatory requirement for all partners. They are standardizing how partner-driven consumption is measured across EC2, S3, RDS, ECS, Lambda, and dozens of other core AWS services.

PRM is not an option; it is the mechanism of record.

Miss the deadline and the consequences are immediate:

  • Loss of AWS Partner Funding Benefits (MDF/SDF)
  • Removal of the "Deployed on AWS" badge
  • Blocked co-sell eligibility with AWS Field teams
  • Freezing of your Marketplace and ACE motions

So what actually is PRM?

PRM is AWS's way of moving from estimated partner influence to actual service‑level attribution. It creates a transparent, data‑backed value exchange between AWS and partners.

AWS attributes revenue through three signals:

  • Zero-touch AWS Marketplace Metering: Automatic attribution for AMI and ML products
  • Resource Tagging: Applying the aws-apn-id tag to cost-incurring AWS resources
  • API Call Decoration: Embedding User Agent strings in your regular AWS API/CLI calls

When your product runs on AWS, PRM creates a measurable signal that tells AWS: this partner is driving real consumption. PRM is the signal that determines whether AWS sellers can actually see you inside their co-sell workflow.

PRM doesn't require Marketplace transactions. Attribution can come from your own AWS accounts, your customers' accounts, or a hybrid of both. The implementation itself typically takes 24-48 hours. AMI, ML, and container workloads must be in customer‑managed accounts. SaaS and Professional Services workloads can be in either partner‑managed or customer‑managed accounts. Success requires the right tagging strategy and environment setup to pass validation.

Why it matters beyond compliance

Compliance is the floor, not the ceiling.

Partners with active PRM data show up differently on AWS scorecards. Field teams can see real consumption data tied to your product. That improves co-sell alignment, strengthens your case for partner funding, and gives PDMs the metrics they need to advocate for you internally.

An ISV without PRM is flying blind inside the AWS ecosystem and increasingly, so is AWS when it comes to seeing your impact.

PRM Implementation Best Practices

To ensure you don't leave revenue credit on the table and avoid compliance drops, follow these three rules:

1. Tag all eligible resource types

AWS PRM tracks over 80+ service types, including EC2 instances, S3 buckets, RDS databases, Lambda functions, EBS volumes, snapshots, and ENIs. Tag every eligible cost-incurring resource to maximize your revenue attribution.

2. Expect the 24-48 hours data delay

After applying tags, it takes 24 to 48 hours for the data to appear in AWS Partner Analytics. Do not panic or alter your configuration if you don't see immediate results.

3. Enable weekly auto-scans

Cloud environments are highly dynamic. Set up automated weekly scans to keep your compliance data up to date. New resources and infrastructure changes can introduce untagged resources at any time, which will silently drain your attribution if left unmonitored.

The DIY Trap: Why Manual Tagging Breaks Infrastructure

Manual PRM execution is an operational minefield. If your engineers handle this themselves, you face three risks:

  • IaC Drift: Manually applying the aws-apn-id tag via the console breaks Terraform, CloudFormation, or CDK states, crashing your next deployment.
  • The "One-Time" Illusion: Cloud environments are dynamic. Without an automated weekly scanning agent, new untagged resources spin up, compliance drops, and revenue attribution leaks.
  • Shadow Resources: PRM supports 88+ services. If you only tag EC2 and S3, but miss ECS Fargate tasks or EBS snapshots, you leave massive revenue credit unclaimed.
